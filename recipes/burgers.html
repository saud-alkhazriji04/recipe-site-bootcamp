Mutton Ghozi Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 whole leg of mutton (about 2 kg)
  • 3 cups basmati rice
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1/2 tsp cloves, ground
  • 4 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • 4 cups mutton broth
  • Salt to taste
  • Fried almonds, raisins, and fresh parsley to garnish

Prep time: 30 minutes  |  Cook time: 2.5 hours

Instructions

  1. Score the mutton leg with deep cuts and rub all over with a mixture of garlic, ginger, turmeric, black pepper, and salt. Let marinate for at least 1 hour.
  2. Heat the ghee in a large heavy pot over medium-high heat. Fry the cumin seeds for 30 seconds until they sizzle. Add the onions and cook until deep golden brown.
  3. Add the marinated mutton leg and sear on all sides until browned, about 8 minutes total.
  4. Add cardamom, cloves, and enough water to cover the meat halfway. Cover the pot and simmer on low heat for 2 hours, turning the meat occasionally, until very tender.
  5. Remove the mutton and set aside. Strain the cooking liquid and measure out 4 cups to use as broth for the rice.
  6. Rinse the rice and add it to the broth in the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover, and cook for 20 minutes until the rice is fluffy.
  7. Serve the rice on a large platter with the mutton leg placed on top. Garnish generously with fried almonds, raisins, and fresh parsley.
